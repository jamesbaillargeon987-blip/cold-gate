OPERATION BRIEF

What Happened Here

In 1958, Soviet geothermal surveyors discovered something in the Siberian bedrock ‚Äî a structure that predated all known human civilization in the region. They built a facility around it. They called it Object 14-V. They called the program Projekt Nasheptyval.

In November 1969, during the final calibration test of a spatial displacement weapon, the fourteen-person research team vanished without a trace. Object 14-V was sealed, classified beyond top secret, and quietly forgotten.

Until three days ago, when a minor seismic tremor shifted the megalith eleven centimeters and reactivated everything.

The players wake in the snow. They were asleep. Now they are in Siberia, in the dark, in ‚àí32¬∞C, with no equipment and a warm patch beneath their feet that is already fading. Someone else was displaced with them. Someone who knows what the facility is for. Someone with a knife and a half-remembered ritual and no intention of waiting.

There are no clean wins here. There is only what shape you are in when it ends.
